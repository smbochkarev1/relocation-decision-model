2 Reading: what the tool does

Read this short B1 text. Hover over dotted words for a quick meaning.

Choosing where to relocate is not easy. Many people who want to move abroad look at several criteria before they decide. This document is a simple tool that helps you compare countries. It gives each country a score, so you can see the results in one table.

The table has a few important columns. Score shows how good a country is in total — a higher score is better. Passport / CBI tells you if you can get a passport by investment. Citizenship shows how fast you can become a citizen; here, “fast” means five years or less. Residence access shows if it is easy or hard to get a residence permit.

The tool also uses simple labels. “Easy + safe” means the country is comfortable and safe. “Open” means people from a country can apply, and “closed” means they cannot. You can click a column to sort the table, or click a row to see more details.

Of course, a tool like this cannot decide for you. It only helps you think clearly. In the end, you must choose the country that fits your life, your job, and your family.

6 Grammar spot: comparing countries

The tool ranks countries, so you need comparatives. Сравнительная степень.

short words: safe → safer, cheap → cheaper  ·  long words: expensive → more expensive, affordable → more affordable  ·  irregular: good → better, bad → worse
Pattern: Portugal is safer than country X.   Visas here are more difficult than before.
